Concept

A multisensory musical visual experience

We want to keep the nature of this process free and alive, as if it were a visit to our subconscious, that deep and hidden place where images and ideas exist in their essential state.

Stripped of any fixed meaning, generating atmospheres that invite the viewer to immerse themselves in different emotions: introspective, trance like, and dance states.

We combine elements of electronic and experimental music with cinematic language and abstract visuals created with artificial intelligence.

開く

hiraku

(from Japanese) It generally means "to open" or "to unfold." It is used to describe the act of opening something, such as a space or a book, or revealing something that was hidden or unknown.

Format / Live Show

Immersive experience

The format of HIRAKU is a live performance, an immersive experience of approximately 90 minutes composed of around six sets per concert.

Each of these sets can be enriched with the participation of special guests who contribute their diverse visions and talents, helping to create a collective and integral discourse that elevates the musical and visual experience to new heights.

About

Federico Carlini

Co-Creator

Musician, art director and visual artist based in Buenos Aires, Argentina. His profile has been shaped by the inevitable attraction between music and the visual arts, with a strong inclination toward improvisation, poetic language and abstract art. He has participated in numerous projects across multiple formats including immersive experiences, virtual reality and AR, music for orchestras, jazz, animation and film. Currently a director at 3dar studios and an independent musician.

Solo album Petricor 2023
Art director for Earth 2018 — Lil Dicky & Leonardo DiCaprio
Director of Paper Birds 2021 — Edward Norton & Joss Stone
Art director for Eggscape 2023 — Venice Immersive Award

Jorge Tereso

Co-Creator

Director and digital artist from Buenos Aires. His work ranges from playful and psychedelic animation to virtual reality experiences and interactive installations. He joined 3dar and became a partner while creating his first award winning short film Shave It. In 2014 he created Light Graffiti, presented multiple times in New York and around the world. Always searching for new ways to expand the possibilities of art and technology.

Co-director of Gloomy Eyes VR 2019 — Sundance & SXSW, Annecy winner
Executive producer on Earth Song by Lil Dicky 2018
Executive producer on Paperbirds 2021
Creative director on Eggscape — Venice Immersive Award 2023
